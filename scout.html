01 — System Overview

What is S.C.O.U.T.?

A fully autonomous aerial monitoring system built specifically for wildlife conservation — combining drone swarms, thermal imaging, and AI detection in one unified platform.

S
Sentinel
Always watching. Drones operate in rotating shifts ensuring continuous, uninterrupted coverage of the entire sanctuary perimeter.
C
Conservation
Purpose-built for wildlife. Every sensor, algorithm, and alert protocol is tuned specifically for endangered animal health detection.
O
Observation
Dual-sensor fusion of RGB and thermal infrared gives rangers and vets a complete picture — visible and invisible to the human eye.
U
Utility
More than a camera — drones can relay GPS coordinates, flag poaching intrusions, and feed live data directly to the donor platform.
T
Terminal
The central dashboard where all data converges — a mission control interface for vets, rangers, and sanctuary managers in real time.

Traditional wildlife monitoring means sending rangers on foot patrols through dense jungle. They cover maybe 15% of a sanctuary per day. A sick tiger in the wrong part of the forest might not be found for 3 days — by which point a treatable infection has become fatal.

S.C.O.U.T. replaces that entirely. A swarm of drones — each equipped with thermal cameras and running our AI health model — covers 100% of the sanctuary continuously. The moment an animal shows signs of distress, the vet is notified.

We're not adding technology on top of existing systems. We're replacing the broken system with something that actually works — and feeding that live data back to donors so every donation is accountable.

S.C.O.U.T. is the field layer of the LEAP Ecosystem — the eyes and ears that make everything else possible.

03 — Swarm Intelligence

No single point
of failure.

Multiple drones operating as one distributed system — self-organising, self-healing, and fully autonomous without human intervention.

🗺️
Coverage Grid Protocol

The sanctuary is divided into dynamic hexagonal zones. Each drone owns a zone and adjusts its patrol path based on real-time coverage gaps reported by the swarm mesh.

🔄
Rotating Shift System

While active drones patrol, resting units recharge at auto-dock stations. The swarm scheduler ensures zero downtime — at least 5 drones are always airborne.

📡
Mesh Relay Network

Each drone acts as a relay node. Data hops between units to reach the base station, maintaining connectivity even in deep jungle terrain where LTE signals fail.

⚠️
Incident Convergence

When a health anomaly is detected, the nearest 2 drones automatically redirect to the subject for multi-angle confirmation — reducing false positives before alerting the vet.

🛡️
Perimeter Defence Mode

Motion classified as human intrusion triggers a perimeter mode — drones redirect to the breach point, record footage, and alert rangers with GPS coordinates in real time.

🧩
Self-Healing Topology

If a drone goes offline or returns to dock, remaining units automatically rebalance coverage zones so no area goes unmonitored. The swarm heals itself in under 90 seconds.

01
Species + Individual ID

YOLOv8 identifies species with 94.2% accuracy, then cross-references individual markings against the sanctuary's animal registry to tag specific animals by name.

02
Posture & Gait Analysis

MediaPipe pose estimation tracks 33 body keypoints per frame. Deviations from a species-specific baseline flag limping, hunching, or abnormal resting positions.

03
Thermal Anomaly Detection

FLIR thermal data is overlaid on the RGB frame. Temperature spikes above species baseline indicate fever, wounds, or inflammation — invisible to standard cameras.

04
Health Score + Protocol

A composite 0–100 health score is generated per animal per pass. Scores below 60 trigger an alert with a suggested vet protocol from our pre-loaded treatment database.
